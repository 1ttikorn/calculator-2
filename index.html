What is BMI (Body Mass Index)?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measure of body fat based on your weight and height. It is a useful indicator of whether you are underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese, and it can provide insights into your overall health.

BMI is calculated using the formula: BMI = weight (kg) / (height (m))^2. The result is then categorized into different ranges to assess your body weight status.

BMI Categories:

  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI 18.5 - 24.9
  • Overweight: BMI 25 - 29.9
  • Obese: BMI 30 or greater

It's important to note that while BMI is a useful screening tool, it may not directly assess body fat percentage or distribution. Consult with a healthcare professional for a comprehensive health assessment.
